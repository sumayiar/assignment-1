Bengal Tiger

The Bengal Tiger is one of the most majestic and iconic big cats in the world. They are the largest tiger subspecies and are known for their striking appearance. Their rich orange coat with black stripes helps them camouflage in their forest habitats. Their native to the the Indian subcontinent, particularly in India, Bangladesh, Nepal, and Bhutan. As mighty carnivores, they prey on deer, wild boars, water buffalos, and other medium to large-sized mammals. They also hunt smaller animals such as monkeys, birds, and reptiles. However, Bengal tigers are listed as "Endangered" on the International Union for Conservation of Nature (IUCN) Red List of Threatened Species.
