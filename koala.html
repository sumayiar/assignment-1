Koala

Koalas are arboreal marsupials native to Australia, recognized by their stout, tailless body and large head with round, fluffy ears. Adults weigh about 9 to 33 pounds and have a thick, woolly fur that ranges from grey to brown, with a white underbelly. Primarily nocturnal, koalas feed on eucalyptus leaves, possessing a highly specialized digestive system to detoxify the poisonous compounds in the leaves. Females give birth to one offspring, called a joey, which spends its first six to seven months inside its mother's pouch. Koalas are solitary animals, spending most of their time asleep among the branches to conserve energy.
